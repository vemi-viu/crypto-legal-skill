# Incorporation for Crypto / Solana Founders

## Why incorporation matters in Web3

Without a legal entity:
- You are personally liable for smart contract exploits, user losses, and regulatory action
- You cannot raise from VCs, sign contracts, or open bank/exchange accounts
- Your contributors have no legal clarity on equity or token allocation

---

## The main structures used by Solana/Web3 founders

### 1. Cayman Islands Foundation Company
**Best for:** Protocol DAOs, token-issuing projects, DeFi protocols

- Separates the protocol from any individual founder legally
- No shareholders — governed by a purpose/mission
- Widely accepted by Tier-1 VCs and exchanges
- Cost: ~$15,000–25,000 to set up via a service provider
- Examples: Most major DeFi protocols use this structure

### 2. British Virgin Islands (BVI) Company
**Best for:** Holding companies, IP ownership, token treasury

- Low cost (~$3,000–5,000), fast setup
- Pairs well with a Cayman Foundation (BVI holds IP, Cayman issues tokens)
- No public registry of shareholders
- Good for teams that want privacy

### 3. UAE — DIFC / ADGM Free Zone
**Best for:** Founders based in Middle East, MENA-facing projects

- ADGM (Abu Dhabi) and DIFC (Dubai) both have clear crypto frameworks
- Can get a real crypto license (VASP)
- Founders can live there and get residency
- Cost: ~$10,000–20,000 depending on license type
- Strong for Pakistani, Indian, Nigerian founders relocating

### 4. Wyoming LLC / DAO LLC
**Best for:** US-based founders, DAOs wanting legal recognition

- Wyoming was first US state to recognize DAOs as LLCs (2021)
- Low cost (~$100–500 to file)
- Members can be token holders
- Risk: US entity = US regulatory exposure (SEC, FinCEN)

### 5. Marshall Islands DAO
**Best for:** Fully decentralized DAOs, no US nexus wanted

- Recognized DAOs as legal entities in 2022
- Very low cost, no physical presence needed
- Increasingly used by DeFi protocols

---

## Recommended stacks by founder type

| Founder profile | Recommended structure |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Raising VC, launching token | Cayman Foundation + BVI OpCo |
| Solo builder, no token yet | Wyoming LLC or simple BVI |
| Based in UAE/Pakistan/MENA | ADGM or DIFC entity |
| Pure DAO, no VCs | Marshall Islands DAO or Wyoming DAO LLC |
| US founder, simple product | Delaware C-Corp (if no token) |

## Step-by-step: setting up a Cayman Foundation

1. Hire a Cayman service provider (Mourant, Maples, Harneys, or boutique providers like Agon or Zuma)
2. Define your foundation's purpose (usually: "advance the [Protocol] ecosystem")
3. Appoint a supervisor (can be a service company)
4. Open a bank or crypto-native account (Silvergate replacement: Mercury, Brex, or crypto-native: Anchorage, Coinbase Prime)
5. Draft a foundation charter and token allocation policy
6. Engage a token counsel to review before any public sale

---

## Red flags to avoid

- Incorporating in your home country if it has hostile crypto regulation
- Mixing personal and company wallets
- Issuing tokens before getting a legal opinion
- Promising token returns to investors without securities counsel
- Using a "DAO" label without actual legal structure — it doesn't protect you
