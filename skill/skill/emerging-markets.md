# Emerging Markets Crypto Legal Guide

Builders from Pakistan, Nigeria, Turkey, India, and the UAE face unique legal landscapes. This file covers what you can and cannot do in each country, and how global founders from these regions structure their projects.

---

## Pakistan 🇵🇰

### Current status (2026)
Pakistan has moved from outright hostility toward a more structured regulatory approach:

- **2018–2023:** State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) banned financial institutions from facilitating crypto transactions
- **2023:** Government began consultations on a crypto regulatory framework
- **2025:** Pakistan Crypto Council established; government signaled intent to legalize and regulate
- **2026:** Draft Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) regulations circulating — not yet enacted

### What Pakistani founders can do right now
- Build crypto products for global markets — no restriction on development
- Accept crypto payments personally or through foreign entities
- Operate a Solana project through a UAE, Cayman, or BVI entity
- Use P2P exchanges to convert crypto to PKR (grey area but widely practiced)

### What is restricted
- Licensed Pakistani banks cannot facilitate crypto transactions (SBP restriction still active as of early 2026)
- No legal framework yet for Pakistani-domiciled crypto businesses
- Offering crypto investment products to Pakistani retail users is legally unclear

### Practical path for Pakistani founders
1. **Incorporate offshore** — UAE (ADGM/DIFC) or BVI is the most common choice
2. **Use a UAE bank account** — Emirates NBD, Mashreq, and WIO Bank are crypto-friendly
3. **Accept payment in USDC/USDT** — convert via Binance P2P or similar to PKR when needed
4. **Do not incorporate in Pakistan** until the VASP framework is enacted and clear

### Tax situation
- Pakistan's FBR (Federal Board of Revenue) has not issued clear crypto tax guidance
- Most founders operating offshore report only when funds enter Pakistan
- Consult a Pakistani tax lawyer before repatriating significant crypto earnings

### Resources
- Pakistan Crypto Council: [@PakCryptoCouncil on X](https://twitter.com/PakCryptoCouncil)
- SECP (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan): secp.gov.pk

## United Arab Emirates 🇦🇪

### Current status (2026) — one of the world's clearest crypto frameworks

The UAE has two main free zones with crypto-specific regulation:

### ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market)
- Regulated by Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA)
- Clear framework for crypto exchanges, brokers, custodians
- Accepts international founders — no UAE nationality requirement
- VASP license: ~$10,000–20,000 + annual fees
- Best for: protocols wanting a real regulated entity in a respected jurisdiction

### DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre)
- Regulated by Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA)
- Added crypto asset framework in 2022
- Strong for financial services and fintech
- DIFC entities widely accepted by global banks

### Dubai mainland — VARA
- Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) regulates crypto on Dubai mainland
- Launched 2022, actively licensing exchanges and crypto businesses
- Binance, Bybit, OKX all hold VARA licenses
- More accessible than ADGM/DIFC for some business types

### What UAE residency gives you
- 0% personal income tax on crypto gains
- Access to UAE banking (crypto-friendly banks available)
- Residency visa through company incorporation
- Gateway to MENA market

### Practical path
1. Incorporate in ADGM or DIFC free zone (~$10,000–20,000)
2. Apply for FSRA/DFSA crypto license if needed
3. Open business bank account (WIO Bank, Emirates NBD, Mashreq)
4. Obtain residency visa through the entity

## Nigeria 🇳🇬

### Current status (2026)
Nigeria has the largest crypto user base in Africa and a rapidly evolving regulatory framework:

- **2021:** Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) banned banks from crypto transactions
- **2023:** CBN reversed the ban; issued new crypto guidance
- **2024:** Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Nigeria launched digital assets framework
- **2025–2026:** VASP registration with SEC Nigeria now available

### What Nigerian founders can do
- Register as a VASP with SEC Nigeria (required for exchanges and custodians)
- Build and operate DeFi/Solana projects for global markets freely
- Accept crypto payments — banks now allowed to process crypto-related transactions
- Incorporate locally or offshore

### Key regulations
- **Investments and Securities Act (ISA) amendment (2024):** Brought digital assets under SEC Nigeria's purview
- **VASP registration:** Required for exchanges, custodians, and brokers serving Nigerian users
- **AML/CFT:** Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU) applies FATF guidelines

### Practical path for Nigerian builders
1. For global products: incorporate in UAE or Cayman, operate globally
2. For Nigeria-focused products: register as VASP with SEC Nigeria
3. Use Grey, Chipper Cash, or Binance P2P for NGN/crypto conversion

## Turkey 🇹🇷

### Current status (2026)
Turkey passed comprehensive crypto legislation in 2024:

- **Crypto Assets Law (2024):** First comprehensive Turkish crypto law
- All crypto service providers must register with Capital Markets Board (SPK)
- AML/KYC requirements now mandatory for all Turkish VASPs
- Turkey added to FATF "grey list" in 2021 — removed after legislation passed

### What Turkish founders can do
- Build products for global markets freely
- Register with SPK if serving Turkish users
- Use licensed Turkish exchanges (Paribu, BtcTurk — both SPK registered)

### Key restrictions
- Crypto cannot be used as a payment instrument in Turkey (cannot pay for goods/services in crypto directly)
- Unregistered VASPs serving Turkish users face fines and criminal liability

### Practical path
- For global products: UAE or Cayman entity
- For Turkey-focused products: register with SPK, implement full KYC

## India 🇮🇳

### Current status (2026)
India has a functioning but restrictive crypto environment:

- **30% flat tax** on crypto gains (no loss offsetting between assets)
- **1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)** on every crypto transaction
- VASPs must register with Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND)
- No ban — crypto is legal but heavily taxed

### What Indian founders can do
- Build products freely — development is unrestricted
- Many Indian founders incorporate in Singapore, UAE, or Cayman to avoid Indian tax burden
- Indian users can use crypto but face heavy taxation

### Practical path
- Most successful Indian Web3 founders incorporate in Singapore or UAE
- Keep development team in India (talent is cheap and excellent)
- Ensure entity is not Indian-controlled (PE risk)
- File ITR with crypto holdings disclosed

## Cross-border operations: common patterns

### "Dubai sandwich" structure (popular for MENA founders)
```
Cayman Foundation (token issuer, IP holder)
        ↓
UAE OpCo (ADGM entity — contracts, banking, hiring)
        ↓
Local team (Pakistan/India/Nigeria — development)
```

### Why this works
- Cayman provides investor-recognized token issuance
- UAE provides real banking, residency, and regulatory clarity
- Local team provides cost-effective talent
- Each layer handles its jurisdiction's requirements
